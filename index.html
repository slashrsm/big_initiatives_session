- big - lasts for years - best talent (already in high demand) - competition for resources - burnout - Let's stop pretending...

Cost of leadership?

- Not discussed much but important - best talent - mentoring - even if other manpower is secured still need leadership - Example: company A hires someone to work on module B... - a lot of non-coding tasks: coordination, sessions, blogs, ...

How to finance?

Volunteer

* we're still relying too much on it

- enthusiasm-driven development - burnout - we burn out best people - they leave/disappear - initiatives die - We complain - We should not

Crowdfunding

- rules - smaller amounts - can spend more $$ with it that you raise - (same people do the organization)

One major commercial backer

- Drupal Commerce - needs solid business model supporting it - needs to solve real-life problems - easier to organize

Distributed corporate funding

- more corporate backers - needs to solve real-life problems - hard to organize/coordinate (cost of leadership!)

Media initative tries to use the latter

Examiner.com, MD Systems, , Gassmann, Letemps, Acquia, NYC Camp, Hubert Burda Media, Ueberbit, ...

- Big, colorful ecosystem - Corporate users (Thunder, Lightning, ...) - No single company that would focus in this area

Contributions need to be driven by business

- some contributions will happen becuse of support and because it is right - but this most often happens from people that already contribute/are involved - bigger contributions will happen only if business needs them - ... and this is validation of our efforts too - But we should not complain about things not done if we don't invest

Drupal businesses need to be responsible

- If your business model depends on Drupal you need to be **actively** involved - not part of this == other people decide your future - 20% back? - It needs to be your mentality, daily routine - If not: you don't use, you abuse
